Text Analysis

For this homework, I didn't know where to start finding texts for comparison. So, I just started opening documents in the intro to DH folder an came across a file named flowersarenot.txt. It was eye-catching because it had a sad energy to it when I opened AntConc to 1 gram. It also had 'Tommy' at the top of the list. I also went ahead and tried finding a similar text with the same vibes called doorway.txt. Both texts are decent in size, Flowers has 9070 words and Doorway has 3641 words. At 2 grams in Flowers, I get so many references of someone named Edward. Doorway has Mrs. Brown at the top, and the words 'don't, can't, won't, and he's' are not far behind. In KWIC the context for 'don't' shows a lot of arguments with Tommy’s parents, and how Tommy is always misbehaving. At the top of 4 grams, is 'couldn't love him.' The context of that phrase showed the title which was "Tommy... Whose Mother Couldn't Love Him."
Of course, I am in complete anguish discovering this. I then switched over to Flowers to 3 grams. The vibe is now hurried and worried. The phrase 'give it up' was at the top. KWIC showed that as a kid, Edward liked getting into mischief and liked picking flowers from his mother’s garden, and how only his mother and his nanny react is how flowers are not made for picking, and to 'give it up.' 'Give it up Edward' is the top of 4 grams. KWIC said the context for it shows that Edward doesn’t want to give up the flowers he's picked, even though everyone says his mother would be angry. It seems that in both texts, the main characters of each story have mothers who were either very cruel or unloving. It's very upsetting but one thing that I've found is that both texts have the word 'don't' near the top of 2 grams, which really shows what kind of childhood these characters had, shown in Flowers Doc 2. It's sad but it's interesting.
